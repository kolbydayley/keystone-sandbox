## üíä DEA Licensure

**Researched:** 2026-01-18 | **Status:** Ready for decision

### TL;DR
DEA registration is $888 per state for 3 years. Paige needs separate registrations for Utah and MA. Also needs state-level CS licenses in each state.

### Cost Summary

| Item | Utah | Massachusetts | Total |
|------|------|---------------|-------|
| DEA Registration | $888 | $888 | $1,776 |
| State CS License | DOPL fee | DPH fee | ~$100-200 |
| Timeline | 4-6 weeks | 4-6 weeks | ‚Äî |

### Required Training
- **MATE Act**: 8 hours of opioid/substance use disorder training (required since 2023)
- Same training counts for both states

### Checklist for Each State

**Utah:**
- [ ] Utah APRN license (verify active)
- [ ] Utah DOPL Controlled Substance license
- [ ] DEA Registration (Utah address)

**Massachusetts:**
- [ ] MA APRN license (verify/apply)
- [ ] MA prescriptive authority
- [ ] MA PMP registration

### Decision Needed

2. Does Paige have active APRN licenses in both states?

## üè• Malpractice Insurance

**Researched:** 2026-01-18 | **Status:** Ready for decision

### TL;DR
Psychiatric NP malpractice insurance runs $1,200-1,800/year. Get an **occurrence policy** (not claims-made) so you don't need expensive tail coverage later.

### Cost Estimates

| Provider | Coverage | Annual Cost |
|----------|----------|-------------|
| NSO/HPSO | $1M/$6M | ~$1,200-1,500 |
| CM&F Group | $1M/$3M | ~$1,421 |
| Berxi | $1M/$3M | ~$1,000 |

### Occurrence vs Claims-Made

| Type | Pros | Cons |
|------|------|------|
| **Occurrence** ‚úÖ | No tail needed, better protection | Costs more |
| Claims-Made | Cheaper initially | Need tail coverage (~2x annual) if you ever cancel |

### Recommendation
Get **occurrence policy** with **$1M/$3M minimum** (ideally $1M/$6M). Budget ~$1,500/year.

### Decision Needed
1. Get quotes from NSO, CM&F, and Berxi?
2. What coverage limit: $1M/$3M or $1M/$6M?
3. Occurrence or claims-made?

### Next Steps (after decision)
- [ ] Request quotes from 3 providers
- [ ] Compare coverage details and exclusions
- [ ] Verify telehealth + multi-state coverage
- [ ] Purchase policy before seeing patients

**Detailed research:** `reference/malpractice-insurance-options.md`

## üìã Insurance Credentialing

**Researched:** 2026-01-18 | **Status:** Ready for decision

### TL;DR
Insurance credentialing takes **3-6 months**. Consider starting cash-pay immediately while credentialing in background.

### Timeline
- CAQH profile: 1-2 weeks
- Payer applications: 2-4 weeks
- Payer processing: 60-120 days each
- **Total: 3-6 months**

### Options

| Approach | Pros | Cons |
|----------|------|------|
| **Cash-pay only** | Start immediately, simple | Limits patients |
| **Insurance** | Broader access | 3-6 month delay, admin burden |
| **Hybrid** ‚≠ê | Best of both | More setup work |

### Recommendation
**Hybrid approach:**
1. Start seeing cash-pay patients immediately
2. Submit credentialing apps in background
3. Add insurance patients once approved

### Decision Needed
1. Insurance, cash-pay, or hybrid?
2. If insurance: Which payers to target?
3. What cash-pay rates to set?

**Detailed research:** `reference/insurance-credentialing-guide.md`
