# Banking & Accounting Setup

**Researched:** 2026-01-18
**Status:** Complete

## Business Banking

### When to Open
After PLLC is formed — need EIN and formation documents.

### Requirements
- EIN (Employer Identification Number) from IRS
- PLLC formation documents
- Operating agreement
- Personal ID for owners

### Recommended Banks

| Bank | Pros | Cons |
|------|------|------|
| **Chase** | Healthcare-specific solutions, widespread | Fees can add up |
| **US Bank** | Healthcare banking tools | Regional |
| **Mercury** | Modern, no fees, easy online | No physical branches |
| **Relay** | Built for small business, no fees | Newer |
| **Local Credit Union** | Personal service, lower fees | Limited features |

### For Multi-State Practice
Choose a national bank (Chase, US Bank) or modern online bank (Mercury, Relay) for easy access in both Utah and MA.

## Accounting Software

### Options

| Software | Monthly Cost | Best For |
|----------|--------------|----------|
| **Wave** | Free | Bootstrapping, simple needs |
| **QuickBooks Self-Employed** | ~$15 | Solo practitioner |
| **QuickBooks Simple Start** | ~$30 | Growing practice |
| **FreshBooks** | ~$19 | Invoice-heavy |
| **Xero** | ~$15 | Alternative to QuickBooks |

### Recommendation
**Start with Wave** (free) or **QuickBooks Simple Start** (~$30/mo). Can upgrade later.

## Payment Processing

### For Patient Payments
Options:
1. **Through ERP** — SimplePractice includes payment processing
2. **Square** — Easy, flat 2.6% + $0.10 per transaction
3. **Stripe** — 2.9% + $0.30, good for online
4. **Ivy Pay** — Built for therapists, HIPAA-compliant

### Recommendation
Use **SimplePractice's built-in** payment processing for simplicity. One system for scheduling, billing, and payments.

## Financial Setup Checklist

### Immediate (after PLLC formed)
- [ ] Get EIN from IRS (free, online, immediate)
- [ ] Open business checking account
- [ ] Get business credit card (for expenses)
- [ ] Set up accounting software

### Before Seeing Patients
- [ ] Connect payment processing
- [ ] Set up invoicing system
- [ ] Create fee schedule
- [ ] Establish superbill template (for insurance reimbursement)

### Ongoing
- [ ] Track all income and expenses
- [ ] Quarterly estimated tax payments
- [ ] Year-end bookkeeping review
- [ ] Consider bookkeeper or accountant

## Tax Considerations

### Quarterly Estimated Taxes
Self-employed must pay quarterly:
- Q1: April 15
- Q2: June 15
- Q3: September 15
- Q4: January 15

### Deductible Expenses
- Malpractice insurance
- ERP software subscription
- Professional licenses and certifications
- Continuing education
- Office supplies
- Home office (if applicable)
- Professional association dues
- Marketing/website costs

### Recommendation
Hire a **CPA familiar with healthcare practices** for tax planning. Worth the investment.
